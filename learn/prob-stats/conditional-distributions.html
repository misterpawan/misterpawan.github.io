Conditional Distributions

Slicing the joint distribution. How our beliefs about X change when we observe Y.

1. Conditional PMF/PDF

The Conditional PDF of \( X \) given \( Y=y \) is essentially a "slice" of the joint PDF at that specific \( y \) value, renormalized so it integrates to 1.

$$ f_{X|Y}(x|y) = \frac{f_{XY}(x,y)}{f_Y(y)} $$

3. Conditional Expectation

The Conditional Expectation \( E[X|Y=y] \) is the mean of the conditional distribution. Since it depends on \( y \), \( E[X|Y] \) is itself a random variable (a function of \( Y \)).

$$ E[X|Y=y] = \int x f_{X|Y}(x|y) \, dx $$

In the interactive example above, notice how the peak (mean) of the conditional distribution shifts as you move \( Y \). That shifting peak traces out the regression line!
