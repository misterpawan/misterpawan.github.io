Moments and Moment Generating Functions

Advanced tools for characterizing probability distributions.

Higher Order Moments

Moments describe the shape of a distribution.

nth Central Moment

The \(n\)th central moment of a random variable \(X\) is defined as: $$ \mu_n = E[(X - E[X])^n] $$

  • 0th Moment: Always 1.
  • 1st Central Moment: Always 0.
  • 2nd Central Moment: Variance (\(\sigma^2\)).
  • 3rd Central Moment: Related to Skewness (asymmetry).
  • 4th Central Moment: Related to Kurtosis (tailedness).

Moment Generating Function (MGF)

The MGF is a powerful tool that, if it exists, uniquely determines the distribution. It can be used to find moments easily.

Definition

$$ M_X(t) = E[e^{tX}] = \sum_x e^{tx} p(x) $$

Generating Moments

We can find the \(n\)th moment (about the origin) by taking the \(n\)th derivative of the MGF and evaluating at \(t=0\).

$$ E[X^n] = M_X^{(n)}(0) $$
  • \( E[X] = M_X'(0) \)
  • \( E[X^2] = M_X''(0) \)
  • \( \text{Var}(X) = M_X''(0) - [M_X'(0)]^2 \)

Examples

Binomial Distribution

For \(X \sim \text{Binomial}(n, p)\): $$ M_X(t) = (pe^t + 1 - p)^n $$

Poisson Distribution

For \(X \sim \text{Poisson}(\lambda)\): $$ M_X(t) = e^{\lambda(e^t - 1)} $$
