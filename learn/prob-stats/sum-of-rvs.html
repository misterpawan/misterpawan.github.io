Sum of Random Variables

What happens when you add two random numbers? The result is a Convolution.

1. Convolution

If \( X \) and \( Y \) are independent random variables, the PDF of their sum \( Z = X + Y \) is the convolution of their individual PDFs.

$$ f_Z(z) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f_X(x) f_Y(z-x) \, dx $$

Intuitively, to get a sum of \( z \), if \( X \) is \( x \), then \( Y \) MUST be \( z-x \). We sum up (integrate) all such possibilities weighted by their probabilities.

3. Key Properties

Expectation is Linear

Always true, even if dependent!

E[X + Y] = E[X] + E[Y]

Variance Adds (if Independent)

Only true if X and Y are independent (or uncorrelated).

Var(X + Y) = Var(X) + Var(Y)
