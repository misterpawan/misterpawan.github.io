Problem 1: Defective Widgets

Binomial

A factory produces widgets where 5% are defective. If you randomly select 10 widgets, what is the probability that exactly 2 are defective?

Solution

Let \(X\) be the number of defective widgets. \(X \sim \text{Binomial}(n=10, p=0.05)\). $$ P(X=2) = \binom{10}{2} (0.05)^2 (0.95)^8 $$ $$ P(X=2) = 45 \times 0.0025 \times 0.6634 \approx 0.0746 $$

Problem 2: Expected Value

Expectation

A game costs $2 to play. You flip a coin. If Heads, you win $5. If Tails, you win nothing. What is your expected net profit?

Solution

Let \(X\) be the winnings. \(P(X=5) = 0.5\), \(P(X=0) = 0.5\). $$ E[\text{Winnings}] = 5(0.5) + 0(0.5) = 2.5 $$ $$ \text{Net Profit} = E[\text{Winnings}] - \text{Cost} = 2.5 - 2 = \$0.50 $$

Problem 3: Poisson Arrivals

Poisson

Emails arrive at a rate of 3 per hour. What is the probability of receiving no emails in a given hour?

Solution

Let \(X \sim \text{Poisson}(\lambda=3)\). $$ P(X=0) = \frac{e^{-3} 3^0}{0!} = e^{-3} \approx 0.0498 $$
