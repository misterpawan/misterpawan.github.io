Law of Large Numbers

Order emerges from chaos. As you collect more data, the sample average converges to the true expectation.

1. The Weak Law

Let \( X_1, X_2, \dots, X_n \) be independent and identically distributed (i.i.d.) random variables with mean \( \mu \). The Sample Mean is: $$ \overline{X}_n = \frac{X_1 + \dots + X_n}{n} $$

$$ \lim_{n \to \infty} P(|\overline{X}_n - \mu| \ge \epsilon) = 0 $$

In simple terms: As \( n \) gets huge, the probability that the sample average is far from the true mean goes to zero.

3. Why it matters

Casinos & Insurance

Casinos don't gamble. They rely on the Law of Large Numbers. An individual player might win, but over millions of hands, the house average is guaranteed.

Scientific Experiments

To measure a physical constant, we repeat the experiment many times and average the results to reduce random noise.
