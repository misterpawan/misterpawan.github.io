Solved Examples

Step-by-step solutions to problems involving continuous random variables, transformations, and conditional probabilities.

1 Finding Constants and Moments

Problem: Consider the PDF of the random variable \( X \):

$$ f_X(x) = \begin{cases} cx^2 & |x| \leq 1 \\ 0 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases} $$
  1. Find the constant \( c \).
  2. Find \( E[X] \) and \( \text{Var}(X) \).
  3. Find \( P(X \geq 1/2) \).

Solution

1. Find \( c \): The total probability must be 1.

$$ 1 = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f_X(x) \, dx = \int_{-1}^1 cx^2 \, dx = c \left[ \frac{x^3}{3} \right]_{-1}^1 = c \left( \frac{1}{3} - \frac{-1}{3} \right) = \frac{2c}{3} $$ $$ \implies c = \frac{3}{2} $$

2. Find \( E[X] \) and \( \text{Var}(X) \):

$$ E[X] = \int_{-1}^1 x \cdot \frac{3}{2}x^2 \, dx = \frac{3}{2} \int_{-1}^1 x^3 \, dx = 0 \quad (\text{Odd function}) $$ $$ E[X^2] = \int_{-1}^1 x^2 \cdot \frac{3}{2}x^2 \, dx = \frac{3}{2} \int_{-1}^1 x^4 \, dx = \frac{3}{2} \left[ \frac{x^5}{5} \right]_{-1}^1 = \frac{3}{2} \cdot \frac{2}{5} = \frac{3}{5} $$ $$ \text{Var}(X) = E[X^2] - (E[X])^2 = \frac{3}{5} - 0 = 0.6 $$

3. Find \( P(X \geq 1/2) \):

$$ P(X \geq 1/2) = \int_{1/2}^1 \frac{3}{2}x^2 \, dx = \frac{3}{2} \left[ \frac{x^3}{3} \right]_{1/2}^1 = \frac{1}{2} \left( 1^3 - (1/2)^3 \right) = \frac{1}{2} \left( 1 - \frac{1}{8} \right) = \frac{7}{16} $$

2 Transformation of Variables (Square)

Problem: Let \( X \) have PDF \( f_X(x) = \frac{1}{2} e^{-|x|} \) for all \( x \in \mathbb{R} \). If \( Y = X^2 \), find the CDF of \( Y \).

Solution

The range of \( Y \) is \( [0, \infty) \). For \( y \ge 0 \):

$$ F_Y(y) = P(Y \le y) = P(X^2 \le y) = P(-\sqrt{y} \le X \le \sqrt{y}) $$ $$ = \int_{-\sqrt{y}}^{\sqrt{y}} \frac{1}{2} e^{-|x|} \, dx $$

Since the integrand is even:

$$ = 2 \int_{0}^{\sqrt{y}} \frac{1}{2} e^{-x} \, dx = \int_{0}^{\sqrt{y}} e^{-x} \, dx = \left[ -e^{-x} \right]_0^{\sqrt{y}} = 1 - e^{-\sqrt{y}} $$

Thus, the CDF is:

$$ F_Y(y) = \begin{cases} 1 - e^{-\sqrt{y}} & y \ge 0 \\ 0 & y < 0 \end{cases} $$

3 Conditional Probability

Problem: Consider \( f_X(x) = 4x^3 \) for \( 0 < x \le 1 \). Find \( P(X \le 2/3 \mid X> 1/3) \).

Solution

Using the definition of conditional probability:

$$ P(A \mid B) = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)} $$ $$ P(X \le 2/3 \mid X > 1/3) = \frac{P(1/3 < X \le 2/3)}{P(X> 1/3)} $$

Calculating the numerator:

$$ \int_{1/3}^{2/3} 4x^3 \, dx = \left[ x^4 \right]_{1/3}^{2/3} = (2/3)^4 - (1/3)^4 = \frac{16}{81} - \frac{1}{81} = \frac{15}{81} $$

Calculating the denominator:

$$ \int_{1/3}^{1} 4x^3 \, dx = \left[ x^4 \right]_{1/3}^{1} = 1^4 - (1/3)^4 = 1 - \frac{1}{81} = \frac{80}{81} $$

Final result:

$$ \frac{15/81}{80/81} = \frac{15}{80} = \frac{3}{16} $$

4 Variance of a Function

Problem: Let \( f_X(x) = x^2(2x + 3/2) \) for \( 0 < x \le 1 \). If \( Y=\frac{2}{X} + 3 \), find \( \text{Var}(Y) \).

Solution

First, simplify the variance expression:

$$ \text{Var}(Y) = \text{Var}\left(\frac{2}{X} + 3\right) = 2^2 \text{Var}\left(\frac{1}{X}\right) = 4 \left( E\left[\frac{1}{X^2}\right] - (E\left[\frac{1}{X}\right])^2 \right) $$

Calculate expectations:

$$ E\left[\frac{1}{X}\right] = \int_0^1 \frac{1}{x} \cdot x^2(2x + 3/2) \, dx = \int_0^1 (2x^2 + \frac{3}{2}x) \, dx = \left[ \frac{2x^3}{3} + \frac{3x^2}{4} \right]_0^1 = \frac{2}{3} + \frac{3}{4} = \frac{17}{12} $$ $$ E\left[\frac{1}{X^2}\right] = \int_0^1 \frac{1}{x^2} \cdot x^2(2x + 3/2) \, dx = \int_0^1 (2x + \frac{3}{2}) \, dx = \left[ x^2 + \frac{3x}{2} \right]_0^1 = 1 + \frac{3}{2} = \frac{5}{2} $$

Calculate variance:

$$ \text{Var}\left(\frac{1}{X}\right) = \frac{5}{2} - \left(\frac{17}{12}\right)^2 = \frac{5}{2} - \frac{289}{144} = \frac{360 - 289}{144} = \frac{71}{144} $$ $$ \text{Var}(Y) = 4 \cdot \frac{71}{144} = \frac{71}{36} $$

5 Transformation of Variables (Sine)

Problem: Let \( X \sim \text{Uniform}(-\pi/2, \pi) \) and \( Y = \sin(X) \). Find \( f_Y(y) \).

Solution

The PDF of \( X \) is \( f_X(x) = \frac{1}{\pi - (-\pi/2)} = \frac{2}{3\pi} \) for \( x \in [-\pi/2, \pi] \).

The range of \( Y \) is \( [-1, 1] \). The transformation \( y = \sin(x) \) is not monotonic on the entire domain.

  • For \( y \in (-1, 0) \), there is one solution \( x \in [-\pi/2, 0] \).
  • For \( y \in (0, 1) \), there are two solutions: \( x_1 \in [0, \pi/2] \) and \( x_2 \in [\pi/2, \pi] \).

Using the transformation formula \( f_Y(y) = \sum \frac{f_X(x)}{|g'(x)|} \) where \( g'(x) = \cos(x) = \sqrt{1-y^2} \):

$$ f_Y(y) = \begin{cases} \frac{2}{3\pi \sqrt{1-y^2}} & -1 < y < 0 \\ \frac{2}{3\pi \sqrt{1-y^2}} + \frac{2}{3\pi \sqrt{1-y^2}}=\frac{4}{3\pi \sqrt{1-y^2}} & 0 < y < 1 \end{cases} $$
