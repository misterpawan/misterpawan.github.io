Problem 1: The Biased Coin

Binomial

A biased coin comes up Heads with probability \( p = 0.6 \). You flip the coin 10 times.
What is the probability of getting exactly 6 Heads?

Solution

Let \( X \) be the number of Heads. \( X \sim \text{Binomial}(n=10, p=0.6) \). The PMF is: $$ P(X=k) = \binom{n}{k} p^k (1-p)^{n-k} $$

Plugging in \( k=6 \): $$ P(X=6) = \binom{10}{6} (0.6)^6 (0.4)^4 $$ $$ P(X=6) = 210 \times 0.046656 \times 0.0256 $$ $$ P(X=6) \approx 0.2508 $$

Problem 2: Expected Winnings

Expectation

You play a game where you roll a fair 6-sided die. If you roll a 6, you win $10. If you roll a 1, you lose $5. Otherwise, you win nothing.
What is your expected winning per game?

Solution

Let \( W \) be the winnings. The possible values for \( W \) are \( \{10, -5, 0\} \). The probabilities are: $$ P(W=10) = P(\text{roll } 6) = 1/6 $$ $$ P(W=-5) = P(\text{roll } 1) = 1/6 $$ $$ P(W=0) = P(\text{roll } 2,3,4,5) = 4/6 $$

The expected value is: $$ E[W] = \sum w \cdot P(W=w) $$ $$ E[W] = 10(1/6) + (-5)(1/6) + 0(4/6) $$ $$ E[W] = \frac{10 - 5}{6} = \frac{5}{6} \approx \$0.83 $$

Problem 3: Call Center

Poisson

A call center receives an average of 4 calls per minute.
What is the probability that they receive exactly 0 calls in a given minute?

Solution

Let \( X \) be the number of calls. \( X \sim \text{Poisson}(\lambda=4) \). The PMF is: $$ P(X=k) = \frac{e^{-\lambda} \lambda^k}{k!} $$

Plugging in \( k=0 \): $$ P(X=0) = \frac{e^{-4} 4^0}{0!} $$ $$ P(X=0) = e^{-4} \approx 0.0183 $$

Problem 4: Variance of a Die

Variance

Find the variance of the outcome of a fair 6-sided die roll.

Solution

Let \( X \) be the outcome. We know \( E[X] = 3.5 \). First, calculate \( E[X^2] \): $$ E[X^2] = \frac{1^2 + 2^2 + 3^2 + 4^2 + 5^2 + 6^2}{6} $$ $$ E[X^2] = \frac{1 + 4 + 9 + 16 + 25 + 36}{6} = \frac{91}{6} \approx 15.167 $$

Now use the variance formula: $$ \text{Var}(X) = E[X^2] - (E[X])^2 $$ $$ \text{Var}(X) = \frac{91}{6} - (3.5)^2 $$ $$ \text{Var}(X) = 15.167 - 12.25 = 2.917 $$
