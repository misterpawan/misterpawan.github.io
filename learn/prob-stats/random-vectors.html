Random Vectors

Generalizing random variables to higher dimensions. Matrices, Covariance, and Multivariate Distributions.

1. Random Vectors & Covariance Matrix

A Random Vector \(\mathbf{X}\) is a column vector where each element is a random variable. $$ \mathbf{X} = \begin{bmatrix} X_1 \\ \vdots \\ X_n \end{bmatrix} $$

The Covariance Matrix \(\Sigma\) captures the variance of each component and the covariance between every pair. $$ \Sigma = E[(\mathbf{X} - \mathbf{\mu})(\mathbf{X} - \mathbf{\mu})^T] $$

2. Multivariate Normal Distribution

The shape of the Multivariate Normal distribution is determined by its Covariance Matrix.
