üîÑ Orthogonal Matrices

An $n \times n$ matrix $Q$ is orthogonal if its columns form an orthonormal set.

$$ Q^T Q = I \quad \implies \quad Q^{-1} = Q^T $$

This means the inverse is just the transpose! Extremely useful computationally.

Principal Axis Theorem

A matrix $A$ is orthogonally diagonalizable ($A = PDP^T$ with $P$ orthogonal) if and only if $A$ is symmetric ($A^T = A$).

Spectral Properties

Real Eigenvalues

Symmetric matrices always have real eigenvalues (no complex numbers needed!).

Orthogonal Eigenvectors

Eigenvectors from different eigenspaces are automatically orthogonal.
